Step 7. Research how malicious extensions can harm users. -
  • Data theft: Extensions can steal sensitive information such as login credentials, browsing history, session cookies, and payment details by secretly monitoring your activity or harvesting data from your clipboard
  • Account compromise: By stealing session cookies, attackers can use them to bypass login screens and take over your accounts on websites like social media or banking portals.
  • Malware and redirects: Extensions can download and install additional malware onto your system or redirect you to phishing websites designed to steal your information.
  • Unwanted ads and tracking: Malicious extensions can inject intrusive and unwanted advertisements into your browsing experience, which not only disrupt usability but also track your online behavior for profit or other malicious purposes.
  • System compromise: In enterprise environments, malicious extensions can provide attackers with a backdoor into a company's network by exploiting the browser as the primary interface for accessing sensitive data and applications.

Summary

Malicious extensions harm users by stealing data like passwords and financial information. So be cautious when installing extensions and only download them from trusted sources, like the official Chrome Web Store. Review the permissions an extension requests before installing and be wary of those that ask for excessive or unnecessary access.
